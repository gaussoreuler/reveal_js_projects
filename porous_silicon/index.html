    Discrepancies and Observations

    Optical response: The wavelength for which the reflectance is highest has been observed to be approx. 860 nm which is far off the target wavelenght of 422 nm. This represents a shift by a factor of approx. 2.1.

    Mirror thickness: The thickness of the mirror was measured to be significantly thicker than planed. 12 alternating layers of thickness 20 nm and 30 nm respectively were indended which should result in a total thickness of 100 nm. The profilometer measurement however, clearly indicates that the thickness of the mirror was approx. 1050 nm. While we could obtain a value for the total thickness, we have no information about the individual layers.

    Hydrogen bubbles inside etch cell: During etching of the mirror the formation of hydrogen bubbles was observed. The generation of hydrogen bubbles is a byproduct of the dissolution reaction.

    Decreasing resistance During etching we observed a steady decrease in electrical resistance of the silicon waver. Does this mean all the layers have different properties?

    Oxidation of porous silicon

    Oxidation after exposure to air: Given its large surface area, the porous silicon layers are highly prone to oxidation when exposed to ambient air.

    Oxidation during etching: Localized oxidation in the course of anodic etching might occur under certain electrochemical conditions, such as insufficient HF concentration or the presence of competing reactions. Under certain electrochemical conditions, such as insufficient HF concentration or the presence of competing reactions, localized oxidation might occur if there's an unintentional introduction of oxidizing conditions. This might be relevant for our case as we used a very low HF-concentration.

    Change of refractive index: Although the pores are filled with a substance with higher refractive index (nSiO2>1), oxidation of porous silicon always decreases its refractive index. That is, it occurs by sacrifice of the silicon fraction with the much higher refractive index nSi=3,86 (λ=633 nm). V.A. Tolmachev E.V. Astrova a. “Effective refractive index and composition of oxidized porous silicon films”. In: Materials Science and Engineering (2000).

    Volume increase caused by oxidation In previous work it has been shown that oxidation does not depend on porosity. \[ V(x) = V_0+V_01.27x\\ \] Therfore, thickness is expected to increase by \[ d/d_0=(1+1.27x)^{1/3} \] For 100% oxidation \((x=1)\) we expect a thickness increase by a factor of approx 1.3. However, at low temperatures, the dependence of the oxidation rate on the crystallo- graphic direction becomes noticeable. Sailor, M.J. (2014). Chemical Reactivity and Surface Chemistry of Porous Silicon. In: Canham, L. (eds) Handbook of Porous Silicon. Springer.

    Conclusions we can draw

    Hydrogen bubbles inhibit formation of pores: Not all hydrogen bubbles reach the surface of the electrolyte. Some stay stuck inside the pores keeping the electrolyte from reaching the bottom of the pores. Hence the dissolution reaction rate is reduced resulting in less pore formation. This has a direct effect on the refractive index of the layers.
    Possible solutions:
    ⇒ Choose lower current density
    ⇒ Shake etch cell (could be dangerous because of HF)
    ⇒ Ultrasonic treatment during etching

    Decreasing resistance: Joule Heating: As the process proceeds, Joule heating can occur due to electrical current flowing through the silicon, affecting resistance and etching rates. This local heating can alter the chemical dynamics of the etching solution and potentially impact uniformity.
    However, the spectrum of the layer stack shows clear characteristics of a dielectric mirror. Therfore, the decreasing resistance could just be a result of decreasing silicon thickness. \(R = ρL/A\), where R is resistance, ρ is resistivity, L is length, and A is the cross-sectional area).
    Possible solutions:
    ⇒ plot avg resistance for each layer
    ⇒ measure temperature

    Oxidation as the driving source for discrepancies: During fabrication oxidation of the porous silicon layers has not been taken into account. Spectrum of the mirror resembles that of a functioning dielectric mirror to reasonable degree. We conclude that all layers have been affected equally (to a reasonable degree). Increase of coss-section of mirror not noticable, oxitation must have been an isotropic. Growth of 2 time inital thickness unlikely however, Error propagation. Therfore, we consider this the main source of error.
    Possible solutions:
    ⇒ Initial thin layer is bound to oxidize fast. Determined etch-rate already wrong.
    ⇒ Determingn etch-rate: Wait until layer has reached its final refractive index value, possibly heat up substrate to speed up full oxidation
